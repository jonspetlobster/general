## Grand Staircase-Escalante (Apr 11–14)

**No permits required.** Dispersed BLM camping is free. F150 4x4 handles all the roads below.

**Drive in (Apr 11):** Hurricane → Kanab (~45 min on US-89), then choose your entry:
- **Paved route:** US-89 → UT-12 north → Escalante (~2 hrs total, always passable)
- **Cottonwood Canyon Road:** Dirt road from US-89 to UT-12 (~2.5 hrs, incredibly scenic, check conditions — avoid after rain)
- **BLM Kanab office for road conditions:** 435-644-1200

| Date | Day | Details |
|------|-----|---------|
| Apr 11 (Sat) | Day 1 | Drive in, set up camp — Hole-in-the-Rock Road dispersed (12 mi south of Escalante) or Cottonwood Canyon Road |
| Apr 12 (Sun) | Day 2 | Devil's Garden hoodoos (easy, great for kids — 13 mi down Hole-in-the-Rock Rd) + Lower Calf Creek Falls hike (5.5 mi RT, waterfall payoff) |
| Apr 13 (Mon) | Day 3 | Peek-a-Boo & Spooky slot canyons (5-6 mi loop, 26 mi down Hole-in-the-Rock Rd) — tight squeezes, kids love it |
| Apr 14 (Tue) | Day 4 | Morning Kodachrome Basin State Park (30 min from Escalante, sandstone chimneys), then drive toward Capitol Reef via UT-12 |

**Fill water in Escalante.** Limited services on Hole-in-the-Rock Road. No gas past Escalante until Boulder.

**UT-12 (Escalante → Boulder → Torrey):** One of America's most scenic roads. Budget extra time — you will stop constantly.

**Gear:** F150 handles all of this easily. Airing down tires not required but nice for the rougher roads.

> ⚠️ **Flash flood warning:** Avoid slot canyons before/during/after rain. Check forecast before heading in.
