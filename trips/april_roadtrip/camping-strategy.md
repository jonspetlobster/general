# Camping Strategy - No Reservations

No reserved campsites on this trip. All dispersed BLM and first-come camping.

## Options for Unreserved Camping

### 1. Dispersed Camping (BLM & Forest Service Land)
**Pros:** Free, abundant, often scenic, solitude  
**Cons:** No facilities (vault toilets sometimes), need to be self-sufficient  

**Rules:**
- Camp 200+ feet from water sources
- Max 14-day stay in one spot
- Pack out all trash
- No campfires during fire restrictions
- Stay on existing roads/sites when possible

### 2. First-Come, First-Serve Campgrounds
**Pros:** Facilities (toilets, sometimes water), established sites  
**Cons:** Can fill up (especially weekends), fees ($10-25/night)  

**Strategy:** Arrive early (before noon) or scout backups

### 3. National Forest Campgrounds
**Pros:** Often less crowded than National Parks, cheaper  
**Cons:** Variable facilities

## Recommended Areas (Santa Cruz → Moab Route)

### California Mountains & Desert

#### Eastern Sierra (Inyo National Forest / BLM)
- **Mountain Biking:** Alabama Hills, Mammoth area trails
- **Camping:** Dispersed BLM around Alabama Hills, Forest Service campgrounds
- **Distance from Santa Cruz:** ~300-350 mi (5-6 hrs)
- **Notes:** Stunning scenery, great for 1-2 nights, might be cold in early April at elevation

#### Death Valley National Park
- **Mountain Biking:** Limited (mostly hiking), but cool to visit
- **Camping:** 
  - Furnace Creek (first-come, fills fast)
  - Texas Spring (first-come)
  - Dispersed camping in surrounding BLM land
- **Distance from Santa Cruz:** ~400 mi (6.5 hrs)
- **Notes:** Can be HOT in April (80-100°F days)

#### Red Rock Canyon / Valley of Fire (Nevada)
- **Mountain Biking:** Some trails, mostly scenic
- **Camping:** 
  - Valley of Fire State Park (first-come sites)
  - BLM dispersed around Red Rock
- **Distance from Santa Cruz:** ~500-550 mi (8 hrs)

### Utah

#### Moab Area (Before/After White Rim)
- **Mountain Biking:** World-class — Slickrock, Porcupine Rim, Mag 7, Whole Enchilada
- **Camping:**
  - Sand Flats Recreation Area (near Slickrock, first-come)
  - BLM dispersed along Highway 128, Potash Road, Lockhart Basin Road
  - Goose Island (along Colorado River, first-come)
- **Notes:** Moab is THE mountain biking destination. Plan extra days here.

#### Capitol Reef National Park
- **Mountain Biking:** Cathedral Valley (4WD), some trails
- **Camping:** 
  - Fruita Campground (first-come)
  - Cathedral Valley dispersed (primitive, 4WD)
- **Distance from Moab:** ~145 mi (2.5 hrs)

#### Goblin Valley State Park
- **Mountain Biking:** Limited, but fun to explore
- **Camping:** First-come sites, often available
- **Distance from Moab:** ~90 mi (1.5 hrs)

#### La Sal Mountains (near Moab)
- **Mountain Biking:** High-elevation trails (might be snowy in early April)
- **Camping:** Forest Service dispersed, Warner Lake campground
- **Notes:** Great if Moab valley is too hot

## Strategy for Your Trip

**Phase 1: CA Mountains/Desert (Apr 4-11)**
- 1-2 nights Eastern Sierra or Desert Parks (route A or B)
- Gooseberry Mesa (Hurricane, UT) riding
- Drive to Grand Staircase-Escalante Apr 11

**Phase 2: Grand Staircase-Escalante (Apr 11-14)**
- Base camp: Hole-in-the-Rock Road dispersed (12 mi south of Escalante town)
  - Backup: Cottonwood Canyon Road dispersed, or Kodachrome Basin State Park (first-come)
- No reservations needed, no permits needed
- Call BLM Kanab for road conditions: 435-644-1200

**Grand Staircase-Escalante highlights:**
- **Devil's Garden** (GSENM) — 13 mi down Hole-in-the-Rock Rd, hoodoo playground, free, great for kids
- **Lower Calf Creek Falls** — 5.5 mi RT, waterfall payoff, very doable for kids
- **Peek-a-Boo & Spooky Gulch** — slot canyon loop, 26 mi down Hole-in-the-Rock Rd, tight squeezes kids love
- **Kodachrome Basin State Park** — easy scenic walks, sandstone chimneys, 30 min from Escalante
- **UT-12** — one of America's most scenic roads (Escalante → Boulder → Torrey → Capitol Reef)

**Road notes for F150 4x4:**
- Hole-in-the-Rock Road: Dirt, washboard, high clearance fine — avoid after heavy rain
- Cottonwood Canyon Road: Dirt, very scenic, check conditions first — impassable when wet
- All other access: Paved (UT-12, US-89)

**Phase 3: Return (Apr 15-18)**
- Capitol Reef (80 mi from Escalante via UT-12) — easy first day back
- Southwest swing or US-50 depending on outbound route
- Return to Santa Cruz Apr 17-18

## Dispersed Camping Apps/Resources

- **FreeRoam** (app) — shows BLM/Forest Service dispersed sites
- **Campendium** — reviews of dispersed sites
- **iOverlander** — user-submitted sites
- **Recreation.gov** — check which campgrounds are first-come
- **Freecampsites.net** — database of free camping

## Gear Notes for Dispersed Camping

- Bring ALL water (5+ gallons)
- Portable toilet or WAG bag
- Trash bags (pack it all out)
- Firewood if allowed (don't gather in desert/fragile areas)
- Solar panel or extra battery for phone/devices
