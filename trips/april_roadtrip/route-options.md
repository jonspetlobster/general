# Route Options - Pre & Post White Rim

*6 rounds of critical review applied: distance pacing, weather risks, biking alignment, camping logistics, first/last day optimization, final polish.*

All routes start/end in **Santa Cruz, CA**.

**White Rim dates (fixed):** April 12–14, 2026  
**Vehicle:** F150 4x4  
**Focus:** Mountain biking first, scenery second

---

## ⚡ TL;DR — Skip to the Answer

**Best trip for mountain biking:**
- **Pre:** Route C (Moab-Centric) — 4–5 riding days on world-class trails
- **White Rim:** Apr 12–14 (fixed) — 3 days of riding

## 🔵 PRE-WHITE RIM ROUTES (Apr 4–11)

You have **8 days** to get from Santa Cruz to White Rim entry at Mineral Bottom/Hwy 313.

---

### ⭐ RECOMMENDED: Route C — Moab-Centric

**Theme:** Maximum mountain biking time on world-class trails  
**Total driving to Moab:** ~990 miles  
**Confirmed riding days:** 4–5 days (+ 3 on White Rim = 7–8 total)  
**Grade: A+**

**TL;DR:** Drive fast, ride more. Two driving days gets you to the best trails on Earth with 4+ days to shred. No scenery detours — that's the trade-off. Totally worth it if riding is the goal.

#### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Reno/Carson City** ✅ PACING: 240 miles, 4 hours
- **Route:** I-80 east through Sacramento/Tahoe corridor
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed, Hungry Valley Road (Carson City area) or budget hotel in Reno
  - *GPS area:* ~39.15°N, 119.75°W — flat BLM land east of Carson City, easy pull-off
  - *Backup:* Motel 6 Reno (~$60) if you want a real sleep before the long haul
- **Why <300 miles:** Relaxed departure. Arrive by 3pm. Cook dinner, prep gear for the brutal Day 2. Do not attempt the full 990 miles in one shot.
- **Activities:** Gas up in Reno, grab groceries at Winco (cheapest in the region), pack cooler

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Reno → Moab** ⚠️ PACING: 750 miles, 11–12 hours
- **Route:** I-80 east → I-15 south (SLC) → I-70 east → US-191 south
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed, Sand Flats Recreation Area ($5/night, staffed gate) or Potash Road (Hwy 279, free, no services)
  - *Sand Flats:* Enter off Mill Creek Drive, ~2 miles from town. First-come. On weekends, arrive by 3pm or it fills.
  - *Potash Road backup:* Hwy 279 south of Moab along Colorado River. Pull off anywhere past mile 5. Free, scenic, quiet.
- **RISK:** This is a brutal drive. You will be tired. Mitigations:
  - Start at 6am. Arrive Moab ~7pm.
  - SLC is your bail point (~540 miles, 7.5 hrs) — hotel if you're cooked
  - Coffee + audiobook plan. Do not push through extreme fatigue.
- **Alternative split (costs 1 riding day):** Reno → SLC (540 mi, Day 2), SLC → Moab (240 mi, Day 3). Only consider this if you're dragging.

**Days 3–6 (Apr 6–9) — Moab: 4 FULL RIDING DAYS** 🚵🚵🚵🚵
- **Camp:** Keep a basecamp — don't move every night. Sand Flats for Days 3–5, then move to Potash or Hwy 128 on Day 6 for variety.
  - *Hwy 128 BLM:* Colorado Riverway, pullouts start at mile 3 heading northeast from Moab. Stunning riverside spots.
  - *Ken's Lake BLM:* South of Moab off Hwy 191. More exposed but solid backup.
- **Water:** Moab has water fill stations at City Market (625 N Main St). Fill up every 1–2 days.
- **Riding schedule:**
  - **Day 3 (Apr 6):** Slickrock Bike Trail — 10 miles, 2–3 hrs, the icon. Start early (8am), hot by noon. Trail fee: $5.
  - **Day 4 (Apr 7):** Porcupine Rim — 15 miles, shuttle to top (shuttle services in town ~$30). Full day. Epic descent.
  - **Day 5 (Apr 8):** Mag 7 or Whole Enchilada (if you want a beatdown). Whole Enchilada requires shuttle (~$35), 26-mile descent from La Sal Mountains — check for snow at trailhead (7,000+ ft) in early April.
  - **Day 6 (Apr 9):** Captain Ahab, Klondike Bluffs, or recovery at Arches NP (walk, don't ride). Your call.
- **⚠️ WIND WARNING:** Moab's windiest season is March–May. Sustained 20–30+ mph days happen. Monitor the forecast. If wind is brutal, the day at Arches (driving/hiking) or a rest/brewery day beats suffering on exposed ridgelines.
- **Services:** City Market for food. Poison Spider Bikes or Chile Pepper for repairs. Eddie McStiff's for post-ride beer.

**Day 7 (Apr 10) — Moab → White Rim Staging Area**
- **Distance:** ~30 miles, 45 min
- **Camp:** Horsethief Campground (BLM, free, on Hwy 313 before Canyonlands turnoff, ~17 miles west of Moab)
  - *Alternative:* Dispersed BLM along Mineral Bottom Road itself (4WD track, test your rig before White Rim)
- **Activities:** Short morning ride (Hymasa/Amasa Back if feeling fresh), then vehicle prep. Pull White Rim gear out, check tire pressure, test 4WD engagement, clean out truck bed.

**Day 8 (Apr 11) — White Rim Final Staging**
- **Camp:** Same spot near Mineral Bottom entry
- **Activities:** Final gear check, pack for 3-day backcountry (water, food, fuel). Early sleep. Entry at Mineral Bottom requires dropping to Canyonlands via a long switchback descent — confirm your 4WD low is working.

#### Scenic Drives (Bonus, No Extra Days Required)
- **Arches Scenic Drive** — 18 miles, do it during a riding rest day
- **Canyonlands Island in the Sky** — Detour on Day 7 driving to staging (adds 45 min)
- **Potash Road (Hwy 279)** — 17 miles along Colorado River, petroglyphs. Beautiful evening drive from camp.
- **La Sal Mountain Loop** — 60-mile alpine loop. Skip it in early April (snow closes upper sections).

#### Weather
- **Moab:** 60–70°F days, 35–45°F nights — ideal spring riding weather
- **Wind:** Peak season. Check weather.gov Moab. Can hit 30+ mph.
- **Rain:** Possible but rare in April. Trails drain fast.
- **Snow:** Only at high elevation (La Sal Mountains, 7,000+ ft)

#### RISKS (Unavoidable)
- **Day 2 is brutal:** 750 miles. No way around it without burning a riding day. You'll be tired that evening.
- **Wind:** Moab can shut down exposed ridgeline riding for a full day. Build in one flexible day.
- **Sand Flats fills:** On weekends, arrive by 3pm or go to Potash/Hwy 128 backup.
- **Whole Enchilada snow:** La Sal access road and upper trail may have snow in early April. Have Mag 7 as a backup plan.

#### Why This Route ✅
- 4–5 full riding days on the best trails in North America
- Best weather of any route (60–70°F, dry, low risk)
- Shortest total driving (990 mi each way)
- You'll be trail-fit and dialed before White Rim
- Simplest logistics — one basecamp for 4 days

### Route A — Eastern Sierra Scenic

⚠️ **NOT RECOMMENDED for mountain biking focus**

**Theme:** High desert + mountain scenery, hot springs, scenic byways  
**Total driving:** ~1,400 miles  
**Confirmed riding days: 2 (possibly 3 if Mammoth cooperates)**  
**Grade: C (scenic: B+, biking: C-)**

**TL;DR:** Beautiful route. Loses 2–3 riding days compared to Route C. Snow at Mammoth (very likely in early April) kills high-elevation riding. Choose this if scenery matters more than saddle time.

#### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Fresno/Visalia Area** ✅ PACING: 180 miles, 3 hours
- **Route:** CA-152 or I-5 south → CA-99
- **Camp:** Hotel in Fresno OR BLM dispersed east of Visalia (Sequoia foothills, free)
- **Why short day:** Original 417-mile Day 1 was way too aggressive for a departure day.

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Fresno → Alabama Hills** ✅ PACING: 180 miles, 3.5 hours
- **Route:** CA-99 → CA-198 → US-395 north
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed Alabama Hills — Movie Road area (free, very accessible, iconic)
  - *GPS:* ~36.60°N, 118.12°W. Park anywhere off Movie Road. Site zero services.
  - *Backup:* Tuttle Creek Campground (fee site, vault toilets, water)
- **Optional add:** Kings Canyon Scenic Byway (+50 mi) — check if open past Grant Grove (often closed until May)

**Day 3 (Apr 6) — Alabama Hills (Ride Day)** 🚵 PACING: 0 miles
- **Camp:** Same spot
- **Activities:** Mountain bike Alabama Hills trails (moderate, fun, desert terrain). Explore Whitney Portal Road. Sunset is spectacular here.
- **Riding assessment:** CONFIRMED riding day. Terrain is rideable, warm, low elevation.

**Day 4 (Apr 7) — Alabama Hills → Mammoth Area** PACING: 120 miles, 2.5 hours
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed, Benton Crossing Road (free, hot springs access)
  - *GPS:* ~37.65°N, 118.85°W. Multiple pull-offs, vault toilet at some sites.

**Day 5 (Apr 8) — Mammoth (Snow-Dependent Day)** PACING: 0 miles
- **Camp:** Same spot
- **Activities:** Hot springs (Wild Willy's, Benton, Crowley Lake area — all accessible, free). Town exploration.
- **⚠️ SNOW WARNING:** Early April = active ski season at Mammoth Mountain. High-elevation MTB trails WILL be buried. Mammoth Bike Park doesn't open until June. Assume ZERO rideable trails. The hot springs are the reason to be here, not riding.
- **Riding assessment:** 0 confirmed riding days. Possible if a freak warm spell clears low-elevation dirt, but don't count on it.

**Day 6 (Apr 9) — Mammoth → Hurricane/Gooseberry Mesa** ⚠️ PACING: 330 miles, 5.5–6 hours
- **Route:** US-395 south → NV-318 east → I-15 south → UT-9
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed, Smithsonian Butte Road south of Hurricane, UT (free, spectacular views)
  - *GPS:* ~37.10°N, 113.28°W. First turnoff south of Hurricane on Smithsonian Butte Rd.
  - *Bail point:* Las Vegas hotels at ~250 miles if fatigued (especially after cold nights at Mammoth)
- **⚠️ PACING NOTE:** 330 miles is over the ideal 300-mile limit, especially after cold camp nights. Start by 8am. Pull over in Las Vegas (Findlay Toyota off I-15, large lot) if you need a break.

**Day 7 (Apr 10) — Gooseberry Mesa (Ride Day)** 🚵 PACING: 0 miles
- **Camp:** Same area
- **Activities:** Mountain bike Gooseberry Mesa — world-class slickrock, Zion views, less crowded than Moab. Trail access is 15 min from camp.
- **Riding assessment:** CONFIRMED riding day. Excellent terrain. One of Utah's best rides.

**Day 8 (Apr 11) — Hurricane → Moab** ✅ PACING: 280 miles, 4.5 hours
- **Route:** I-15 north → I-70 east → US-191 south
- **Camp:** BLM near Horsethief Campground/Mineral Bottom staging

#### Weather
- **Alabama Hills:** 60–75°F days, 35–45°F nights — perfect
- **Mammoth:** 40–55°F days, 20–35°F nights — cold. Snow very likely at elevation.
- **Zion/Hurricane:** 65–75°F days, 40–50°F nights — great
- **Moab:** 60–70°F days, 35–45°F nights — great

#### RISKS (Unavoidable)
- **Mammoth WILL have snow in early April.** Ski resort is still running. Plan for 0 rideable MTB days there.
- **Kings Canyon road** likely closed past Grant Grove. Check CalTrans before departure.
- **Day 6 is 330 miles** after cold Mammoth camp nights. Start early, bail to Vegas if tired.
- **Only 2 confirmed riding days** (Alabama Hills + Gooseberry) vs. Route C's 4–5 days.

#### Why Choose This Route:
✅ Scenic variety (Sierra Nevada, high desert, canyon country)  
✅ Hot springs (Wild Willy's, Benton — excellent even in snow season)  
✅ Less crowded than southern route  
❌ Snow kills Mammoth riding (certain)  
❌ Lose 2–3 riding days vs. Route C  
❌ Day 6 pacing is tight  

**Verdict:** Choose this if you prioritize landscape variety and hot springs over riding volume. Not recommended for biking-focused trip.

### Route B — Desert Parks Scenic

⚠️ **NOT RECOMMENDED for mountain biking focus**

**Theme:** Iconic desert parks, scenic drives, limited biking  
**Total driving:** ~1,250 miles  
**Confirmed riding days: 1–2**  
**Grade: C+ (scenic: A, biking: D+)**

**TL;DR:** Best scenery of any route. Death Valley and Valley of Fire are stunning. But Death Valley biking is essentially zero (wrong terrain, dangerous heat). You get one good riding day at Gooseberry. That's it.

#### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Bakersfield Area** ✅ PACING: 230 miles, 3.5 hours
- **Route:** I-5 south → CA-58 east
- **Camp:** Hotel in Bakersfield OR BLM dispersed east of town (free)
- **Why short day:** Original 400-mile Day 1 was too aggressive. And arriving Death Valley in afternoon heat is a bad start.

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Bakersfield → Death Valley** ✅ PACING: 170 miles, 3 hours
- **Route:** CA-58 → CA-178 into Death Valley
- **Camp:** Texas Spring Campground (first-come, $16/night) OR BLM dispersed near Beatty, NV (~20 miles outside park, free, cooler temps)
  - *Beatty BLM:* Much cooler at night than in-valley camping. Easier to sleep.
- **⚠️ HEAT WARNING:** Even arriving at 1pm, ambient temp will be 85–90°F. Shade up, drink immediately.

**Day 3 (Apr 6) — Death Valley** ⚠️ 0 miles (heat management day)
- **Camp:** Same spot
- **Activities:** 5am–9am ONLY for outdoor activity (Mesquite Dunes, Zabriskie Point sunrise). Retreat to shade or A/C by 10am. Evening: Artist's Drive, Golden Canyon hike after 5pm.
- **⚠️ HEAT WARNING:** Afternoon temps hit 90–100°F+ in early April. This is not hyperbole — people die here. No hiking or biking during midday.
- **Riding assessment:** 0 riding days. Death Valley is wrong terrain for MTB and too hot.

**Day 4 (Apr 7) — Death Valley → Valley of Fire** ✅ PACING: 160 miles, 2.5 hours
- **Route:** Through Beatty/Pahrump → Valley of Fire
- **Camp:** Valley of Fire State Park (first-come, $25–30/night) or BLM dispersed nearby
  - *BLM backup:* Muddy Mountains Wilderness north of Valley of Fire, free
- **Activities:** Afternoon exploration, petroglyph hikes

**Day 5 (Apr 8) — Valley of Fire → Hurricane/Gooseberry** ✅ PACING: 135 miles, 2 hours
- **Camp:** BLM, Smithsonian Butte Road (free, views of Zion from camp)

**Day 6 (Apr 9) — Gooseberry Mesa (Ride Day)** 🚵 PACING: 0 miles
- **Camp:** Same spot
- **Activities:** Mountain bike Gooseberry Mesa. World-class slickrock, 15 miles of trail, Zion views. CONFIRMED riding day.
- **Riding assessment:** 1 confirmed riding day.

**Day 7 (Apr 10) — Hurricane → Moab** ✅ PACING: 280 miles, 4.5 hours
- **Camp:** BLM near Moab, Horsethief area

**Day 8 (Apr 11) — Moab Staging** PACING: minimal
- **Camp:** Move closer to Mineral Bottom
- **Activities:** Light morning ride (Hymasa), White Rim prep

#### Weather
- **Death Valley:** 80–100°F days, 55–65°F nights — dangerously hot by midday
- **Valley of Fire:** 75–85°F days, 50–60°F nights
- **Hurricane/Zion:** 65–75°F days, 40–50°F nights
- **Moab:** 60–70°F days, 35–45°F nights

#### RISKS (Unavoidable)
- **Death Valley WILL be dangerous midday.** No joking. 100°F+ kills. Early morning ONLY.
- **0 Mountain biking at Death Valley** — wrong terrain, too hot, no trails.
- **Only 1–2 confirmed riding days** (Gooseberry, and maybe a quick Moab ride on Day 8).
- **Valley of Fire first-come camping** fills on weekends and holidays.

#### Why Choose This Route:
✅ Iconic desert parks (Death Valley, Valley of Fire — stunning photography)  
✅ Warm/sunny weather  
✅ Excellent scenic drives  
❌ Death Valley is not rideable  
❌ Only 1–2 confirmed riding days  
❌ Daily heat management required in Death Valley  

**Verdict:** Perfect trip if you want desert photography and iconic landscapes. Wrong choice if riding is the goal.

### ❌ Route D — Idaho High Desert Loop (DO NOT RECOMMEND)

**Skip this route. Here's why:**

- **Longest drive:** 1,400+ miles (400 more miles than Route C)
- **Coldest weather:** 20–35°F nights in Idaho high desert in early April. Craters of the Moon sits at ~5,900 ft elevation. Possible snow.
- **Least riding:** 1–2 days at most. City of Rocks is a rock climbing area, not an MTB destination. Craters of the Moon has minimal trails.
- **You arrive exhausted:** After 1,400 miles of driving through Idaho, you'll be grinding into Moab on Apr 10–11 with zero riding days before White Rim.

**The honest verdict:** Idaho is a cool trip — for summer. Early April is too cold, the scenery payoff doesn't offset the mileage, and it's not aligned with mountain biking. Save it for a standalone trip in June–September when Sun Valley, Soldier Mountain, and Wood River trails are prime.

*No itinerary provided. It's not competitive.*

## 🟢 POST-WHITE RIM ROUTES (Apr 15–18)

You have **4 days** to return to Santa Cruz after exiting White Rim on Apr 14.

**Reality check:** It's ~990 miles from Moab to Santa Cruz. There is no shortcut through Nevada. You WILL spend 2 days covering 700–800 miles when you're exhausted from 3 days on White Rim. The question is whether you rest first or push straight through.

---

### ⭐ RECOMMENDED: Route B — Moab Recovery + Direct Return

**Theme:** Rest, recover, then drive home efficiently  
**Total driving:** ~990 miles  
**Recovery days:** 2 full days  
**Grade: A**

**TL;DR:** Get a hotel in Moab. Shower. Sleep. Eat a real meal. Spend Day 2 at a brewery or getting a massage. Then rip home. You'll be tired from White Rim — don't be a hero.

#### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 15) — Exit White Rim → Moab** ✅ PACING: 30 miles, 1 hour
- **Exit:** Shafer Trail exit → Hwy 191 back to Moab
- **Lodging:** **HOTEL IN MOAB.** Not a campsite. Hotel.
  - *Recommendations:* Hoodoo Moab (full amenities, pool), SpringHill Suites, or Budget Inn (cheap, clean)
  - *Why hotel:* After 3 days on White Rim without a shower, you'll be filthy, sore, and depleted. A $100 hotel room is the best money you'll spend on this trip.
- **Activities:** Shower (twice), eat real food (Moab Brewery, Pasta Jay's), sleep by 9pm

**Day 2 (Apr 16) — Moab Full Recovery**
- **Lodging:** Same hotel OR move to BLM dispersed if budget-conscious
- **Activities:**
  - Morning: Spa/massage (Moab has several)
  - Afternoon: Resupply, laundry, coffee shop, gear pack for drive home
  - Evening: Light ride on Bar M Loop (easy flat trail, 8 miles, fun even on tired legs) if feeling human
- **⚠️ Note:** Do NOT go back to Slickrock or Porcupine the day after White Rim. Your body needs the break.

**Day 3 (Apr 17) — Moab → Ely, Nevada** ⚠️ PACING: 350 miles, 5.5 hours
- **Route:** US-191 north → I-70 west → US-50 west ("Loneliest Road in America")
- **Lodging:** Hotel in Ely, NV (Hotel Nevada — historic, cheap, good) OR BLM dispersed near Ely
  - *BLM spot:* Ward Mountain area west of Ely, free, piñon-juniper forest
- **⚠️ PACING NOTE:** 350 miles is at the limit when post-White Rim tired. Start by 8am. US-50 across Nevada is remote — fill gas in Delta, UT (right off I-70) and again in Ely.
- **⚠️ REMOTE STRETCH:** US-50 Nevada has 250+ miles between services. Do not assume gas stations are open. Carry an extra 5-gallon can.

**Day 4 (Apr 18) — Ely → Santa Cruz** ⚠️ PACING: 400 miles, 6–6.5 hours
- **Route:** US-50 west → I-80 west → home
- **⚠️ PACING NOTE:** 400 miles when exhausted is the hardest stretch of the whole trip. Options:
  - *Option A:* One shot — start 7am, arrive Santa Cruz ~2pm. Doable if you slept well.
  - *Option B (better):* Ely → Sacramento (350 mi), sleep at motel, then Sacramento → Santa Cruz (90 mi) next morning. Easy. Only costs 1 extra day.
  - *Recommendation:* If you have any flexibility, do Option B. The extra $80 motel night is worth it.

**Alternative 5-Day Return (best pacing, requires extending trip to Apr 19):**
- Day 1 (Apr 15): White Rim exit → Moab hotel (30 mi)
- Day 2 (Apr 16): Full recovery day in Moab
- Day 3 (Apr 17): Moab → Ely (350 mi)
- Day 4 (Apr 18): Ely → Sacramento (350 mi)
- Day 5 (Apr 19): Sacramento → Santa Cruz (90 mi) — easy morning drive home

This 5-day version keeps every driving day under 350 miles and never pushes past 6 hours. Strongly recommended if your schedule allows.

#### RISKS (Unavoidable)
- **Days 3–4 are brutal when you're post-White Rim tired.** Factor in fatigue.
- **US-50 is remote:** No services for 250+ miles in Nevada. Gas in Delta, UT and Ely, NV. Carry extra fuel.
- **Day 4 (400 mi) is risky if you're running on empty.** The Sacramento split is the smart play.

#### Why This Route:
✅ 2 full recovery days post-White Rim  
✅ Hotel = shower, real bed, A/C, actual sleep  
✅ Simplest return route (no navigation required, straight shot)  
✅ Mental and physical reset before 2 long drive days  
❌ Two driving days are still long (geography, no shortcut)
