## ⭐ RECOMMENDED PRE-TRIP: Route C — Moab-Centric (Maximum Ride Time)

![Moab Landscape](images/moab-landscape.jpg)
*Canyonlands area, Utah — Wikimedia Commons*

### TL;DR
Drive to Moab in 3 comfortable days (no day over 350 miles), then ride world-class trails for 4 full days before White Rim. Best weather, least driving stress, most saddle time. **This is the right call for a mountain biking trip.**

**Biking Grade: A+** | **Riding Days: 4–5** | **Total Miles: ~990**

### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Reno** ✅ DEPARTURE DAY
- **Distance:** 240 miles | 4 hours
- **Route:** I-80 east through Sacramento, Donner Pass
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed east of Reno near Pyramid Lake (Sutcliffe area, BLM Winnemucca district) OR motel in Reno/Sparks
- **Gas:** Plenty of stations; fill in Sacramento for backup
- **Activities:** Relaxed start, arrive by early evening, gear check
- **Note:** Don't try to push further. 240 miles is the right call — you're fresh tomorrow.

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Reno → Ely, NV**
- **Distance:** 295 miles | 4.5 hours
- **Route:** I-80 east → US-93 south → US-50 east into Ely
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed south of Ely on US-93 (pulloffs near Cave Lake area, ~5 miles south of town) OR Budget Inn Ely (~$70)
- **Gas:** Fill in Reno, next reliable stop is Ely (295 miles). Carry a 5-gallon jerry can OR fill at Battle Mountain (I-80 junction) if taking northern route.
- **💧 WATER WARNING:** 200+ miles of remote Nevada with no services between Winnemucca and Ely. Carry 3 gallons per person.
- **Activities:** Drive day. Audiobooks. Arrive early evening.

**Day 3 (Apr 6) — Ely → Moab**
- **Distance:** 295 miles | 4.5 hours
- **Route:** US-6 east → US-50 east (crossing Utah border) → UT-21 → I-70 east → US-191 south
- **Camp:** **BLM Hwy 128 corridor** (Colorado River, free — pulloffs at mile markers 10–22, best spots near Big Bend at mm 14) OR **Potash Road (Hwy 279)** dispersed (petroglyphs at mile 5, right on the river, free)
- **Gas:** Fill in Ely. Next reliable gas: Delta UT (105 miles from Ely junction), then Moab (100 more miles).
- **💧 WATER WARNING:** Ely to Delta UT = 145 miles, limited services. Carry 3+ gallons.
- **Activities:** Arrive Moab by early-to-mid afternoon. Evening ride possible — try **Bar M Loop** (🟢 Easy, 7.8 mi) to shake off the drive.

> **Alternative 2-Day Drive Option:** Day 1 → Reno (240mi), Day 2 → Moab (750mi). ⚠️ **Day 2 is brutal and not recommended.** You arrive exhausted for your first riding day. The 3-day split gets you to Moab equally fresh with no penalty to riding days.

---

**Days 4–7 (Apr 7–10) — Moab: 4 FULL RIDING DAYS** 🚵🚵🚵🚵

- **Camp options (rotate through all three):**
  - **Sand Flats Recreation Area:** $20/night fee, pit toilets, right at Slickrock trailhead (1.5 mi from town on Sand Flats Rd). **Fills Thursday afternoon on weekends — arrive mid-week.** Best for Days 4–5.
  - **Hwy 128 (Colorado River Corridor):** Free BLM dispersed, various pulloffs mm 8–28. No facilities. River access. Best for Days 5–6.
  - **Potash Road (Hwy 279):** Free BLM dispersed, first 5 miles from Moab. Petroglyphs nearby. No facilities.
  - **Backup:** Ken's Lake area (BLM dispersed off US-191 south, 3 miles from Moab, fire rings at some sites)

- **Riding Schedule:**

  | Day | Trail | Difficulty | Distance | Notes |
  |-----|-------|-----------|---------|-------|
  | Day 4 (Apr 7) | **Slickrock Trail** | ⬛ Expert | 10.5 mi loop | Iconic. Deceptively hard. Start with the 0.8-mi Practice Loop. |
  | Day 5 (Apr 8) | **Porcupine Rim** | ⬛ Expert | 15.5 mi (shuttle) | Best descent in Moab. Arrange shuttle day before (Moab Cyclery or DIY). |
  | Day 6 (Apr 9) | **Mag 7** or **Captain Ahab** | ⬛ Expert / 🔵 Intermediate | 15 mi / 4.9 mi | Mag 7 for advanced riders; Captain Ahab for a flow day. Both excellent. |
  | Day 7 (Apr 10) | **Klondike Bluffs** or **Dead Horse Trail System** | 🔵 Intermediate | 9 mi / 5–12 mi | Dinosaur tracks at Klondike. Lower-key day before staging. |

- **Services in Moab:** Moab Cyclery (bike rentals/repairs), City Market (resupply), multiple breweries (Moab Brewery, Under the Stars Brewing), Moab Diner

- **Weather contingency:** If strong wind (>25mph), skip exposed mesa rides (Porcupine). Do Captain Ahab or Klondike (more sheltered). Check [Mountain Project Moab](https://www.mountainproject.com) for conditions.

---

**Day 8 (Apr 11) — White Rim Staging**
- **Distance:** ~15 miles from Moab | 30 minutes
- **Camp:** **BLM dispersed along Hwy 313** — take Hwy 313 (Dead Horse Point Road) ~18 miles from US-191 turnoff. Multiple pulloffs before the Dead Horse Point junction. You're now ~12–15 miles from Mineral Bottom Road entry. ✅ Within 30-mile requirement.
  - **Alternative:** Horsethief Campground area — BLM dispersed sites off a dirt spur ~2 miles past the Hwy 313/Dead Horse Point junction, right above the canyon rim. Zero facilities, zero fee.
- **💧 WATER WARNING:** No water at Mineral Bottom or on Hwy 313. Fill all tanks in Moab before staging. Carry all White Rim water from here.
- **Gas:** Fill in Moab — no gas on Hwy 313 or at White Rim entry.
- **Activities:** Light morning ride (Hymasa: ⬛ Advanced, 6.4 mi) OR rest legs. Afternoon: vehicle prep, 4WD test, pack White Rim gear, early sleep.

## Route A: Eastern Sierra Scenic — SCENIC ROUTE ⚠️ Not Optimized for Biking

![Alabama Hills](images/alabama-hills.jpg)
*Alabama Hills, California — BLM official photo*

> **SCENIC ROUTE — NOT OPTIMIZED FOR MOUNTAIN BIKING.** Expect 2–3 riding days (vs. Route C's 4–5), significant weather uncertainty at Mammoth, and more total driving. Choose this for scenery and hot springs, not trail time.

### TL;DR
High desert and mountain scenery through the Eastern Sierra. Alabama Hills, Mammoth Lakes hot springs, Zion slickrock. Beautiful but snow will likely shut down Mammoth biking in early April. **2–3 actual riding days.** Not the right call if riding is the priority.

**Biking Grade: D+** | **Riding Days: 2–3** | **Total Miles: ~1,350**

### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Fresno/Visalia** ✅ DEPARTURE DAY
- **Distance:** 180 miles | 3 hours
- **Route:** CA-152 or I-5 south
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed east of Visalia (foothill roads off CA-198, elevation 1,000–2,000 ft) OR motel in Fresno/Visalia
- **Activities:** Easy start, arrive by afternoon

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Fresno → Alabama Hills**
- **Distance:** 180 miles | 3.5 hours
- **Route:** CA-99 south → CA-198 east → US-395 north to Lone Pine
- **Optional:** Kings Canyon Scenic Byway (+50 mi, +2 hrs) via CA-180. **Check before leaving:** this road is often gated past Grant Grove until late May. Call Sequoia/Kings Canyon NP: 559-565-3341.
- **Camp:**
  - **Movie Road dispersed (Alabama Hills):** Free BLM, pull off Movie Road (off Whitney Portal Rd), many sites spread across the flats. No fee, no facilities.
  - **Tuttle Creek Campground (BLM):** $8/night, piped water (Apr–Oct), fire rings. 3 miles west of Lone Pine off Horseshoe Meadow Rd.
- **Gas:** Fill in Fresno or Visalia. Lone Pine has gas. Bishop (40 miles north) has full services.
- **Activities:** Late afternoon setup, golden-hour exploration of the rock formations

**Day 3 (Apr 6) — Alabama Hills Riding Day** 🚵
- **Camp:** Same spot
- **Trail:** Alabama Hills 2-track/OHV riding (🔵 Moderate, no dedicated MTB singletrack). Loose decomposed granite, fun exploration but not Moab-level. Combine with Meysan Lake area lower trails. Best suited for hardtail.
- **Also:** Hike Whitney Portal Road (13 miles, Mt. Whitney views), arch hunting on foot
- **Weather:** 60–75°F days, 35–45°F nights. Dry. Ideal.

**Day 4 (Apr 7) — Alabama Hills → Mammoth Area**
- **Distance:** 120 miles | 2.5 hours
- **Route:** US-395 north
- **Camp:**
  - **Benton Crossing Road dispersed:** Off US-395 ~7 miles north of Bishop turnoff, elevation ~4,800 ft (BELOW snow line). Free BLM, no facilities. Best option to avoid snow at camp.
  - **Glass Creek dispersed:** North of Mammoth off US-395, ~7,300 ft elevation. May have snow at campsite in early April.
- **Gas:** Bishop (full services, fill here)

**Day 5 (Apr 8) — Mammoth Lakes** 🚵 (weather-dependent)
- **Camp:** Same spot

> ⚠️ **MAMMOTH WEATHER WARNING — READ THIS:**
> Early April = snow IS the norm at elevation. Mammoth Mountain ski resort is typically open through April. Trails above 7,000 ft are **likely snow-covered and unrideable.** Don't plan around Mammoth biking. Plan around it being a hot-springs-and-hike day with biking as a bonus IF conditions allow.

- **Biking IF trails are rideable (below 7,000 ft):**
  - Lower Kamikaze (🔵 Intermediate, dirt road area near Main Lodge) — may be clear
  - Lower Sherwins trails — sometimes rideable early season
  - **Check:** [Mammoth Mountain trail conditions](https://www.mammothmountain.com/mountain-biking) before arriving
- **If snow closes trails (likely):** Wild Willy's Hot Springs (free, off Benton Crossing Rd), Benton Hot Springs ($25/person), town coffee, explore Mammoth Creek
- **Contingency:** If you arrive to snow and closed trails, don't waste a day — push toward Cedar City/Hurricane area a day early.
- **Weather:** 40–55°F days, **20–35°F nights**. Dress accordingly. Snow at camp possible.

**Day 6 (Apr 9) — Mammoth → Cedar City/Hurricane Area** ⚠️ LONG DAY
- **Distance:** 330 miles | 5.5–6 hours
- **Route:** US-395 south → CA-14 → I-15 north → UT-9 west OR NV-318 (more scenic, empty highway through Nevada)
- **Camp:**
  - **Smithsonian Butte Road dispersed (Hurricane, UT):** Off UT-59 south of Hurricane, 5-mile dirt road to Canaan Mountain BLM. Free, no facilities, views of Zion.
  - **Warner Valley Road dispersed:** BLM south of Hurricane, easy 2WD access. Free.
- **Gas:** Bishop (fill before leaving Mammoth area). Tonopah NV (300 miles later) if taking NV-318. Cedar City UT (full services).
- **⚠️ WARNING:** 330 miles after a cold night and potentially disappointing (snow) biking day. Consider stopping at Las Vegas (~250 miles from Mammoth) if morale is low.

**Day 7 (Apr 10) — Gooseberry Mesa Riding Day** 🚵
- **Camp:** Same spot (Hurricane/Gooseberry area)
- **Trail:** **Gooseberry Mesa** (⬛ Expert/Black Diamond, 9-mile loop). World-class slickrock, less crowded than Moab. One of the top 10 MTB destinations in the Southwest.
  - Access: Gooseberry Mesa Road off UT-59, 10 miles west of Hurricane. High-clearance recommended (doable in F150).
  - **Check:** Road conditions after rain — can be muddy. Call BLM St. George: 435-688-3200.
- **Also:** Zion Canyon Scenic Drive (shuttle required in peak season), Kolob Canyons

**Day 8 (Apr 11) — Hurricane → White Rim Staging**
- **Distance:** 280 miles | 4.5 hours
- **Route:** I-15 north → I-70 east → US-191 south → Hwy 313
- **Camp:** BLM dispersed along Hwy 313 (see Route C Day 8 staging notes above)
- **Gas:** Fill in Cedar City or St. George. Next gas: Moab (135 miles from I-70/US-191 junction).

## Route B: Desert Parks Scenic — SCENIC ROUTE ⚠️ Not Optimized for Biking

![Death Valley](images/death-valley.jpg)
*Death Valley National Park — Wikimedia Commons*

> **SCENIC ROUTE — NOT OPTIMIZED FOR MOUNTAIN BIKING.** Death Valley has no real MTB. Gooseberry is 1 day of riding. Total: 1–2 riding days. Choose this for iconic desert parks, not trail miles.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 4) — Santa Cruz → Bakersfield** ✅ DEPARTURE DAY
- **Distance:** 230 miles | 3.5–4 hours
- **Route:** I-5 south → CA-58 east
- **Camp:** Motel in Bakersfield OR BLM dispersed in foothills east of town (Tehachapi area, Bear Valley Springs BLM)
- **Activities:** Easy departure

> **⚠️ Why not drive to Death Valley on Day 1?** The original route had 400 miles on Day 1 AND arriving Death Valley in afternoon heat. Both are bad. Stage in Bakersfield.

**Day 2 (Apr 5) — Bakersfield → Death Valley**
- **Distance:** 170 miles | 3 hours
- **Route:** CA-58 east → CA-178 into Death Valley (Panamint Valley approach)
- **Camp:**
  - **Texas Spring Campground (Death Valley NP):** $14/night, first-come. Fills **Friday afternoons on weekends** during spring. Near Furnace Creek, shade trees, some facilities.
  - **Sunset Campground:** Larger, $14/night, more exposed. Better availability backup.
  - **Beatty, NV BLM dispersed:** Exit park on NV-374, dispersed sites off side roads ~3 miles from Beatty. Free, no facilities. Cooler at night (3,300 ft vs. Furnace Creek at -282 ft).
- **Gas:** Fill in Bakersfield. Pahrump NV (if routing that way) or Furnace Creek (in-park, expensive). **Do not rely on Death Valley gas — carry 5+ gallons extra.**
- **💧 WATER WARNING:** Carry 6+ gallons per person for Death Valley. This is not a suggestion.
- **Activities:** Arrive before noon. Badwater Basin (morning or evening only). Zabriskie Point sunrise/sunset.

**Day 3 (Apr 6) — Death Valley Rest Day**
- **Camp:** Same spot

> ⚠️ **DEATH VALLEY HEAT WARNING — READ THIS:**
> Early April afternoon temps routinely hit **90–100°F+ at valley floor.** This is not hot-but-manageable; this is dangerous. All activities must be completed before 11am or after 5pm. Midday = water, shade, rest.
>
> **There is no mountain biking at Death Valley.** Wrong terrain, wrong temperatures. This is a hiking/photography park.

- **Morning (5am–11am):** Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes (sunrise), Artist's Drive, Golden Canyon hike
- **Midday (11am–5pm):** Car A/C, visitor center (A/C), Furnace Creek Inn pool (day pass available), read, sleep
- **Evening (5pm–sunset):** Dante's View (5,476 ft, noticeably cooler), Zabriskie Point sunset, star gazing

**Day 4 (Apr 7) — Death Valley → Valley of Fire**

![Valley of Fire](images/valley-of-fire.jpg)

- **Distance:** 160 miles | 2.5–3 hours

  - **Arch Rock Campground (Valley of Fire SP):** $35/night, more private. Same fill times.
  - **BLM dispersed outside park:** Exit on NV-169 east, dispersed sites in BLM land east of park. Free, no facilities.
- **Gas:** Fill in Beatty NV or Pahrump NV. Las Vegas is 50 miles from Valley of Fire (full services).
- **Activities:** Arrive afternoon. White Domes, Elephant Rock, Fire Wave hike

**Day 5 (Apr 8) — Valley of Fire → Hurricane/Gooseberry Area**

*Zion National Park — NPS official photo*

- **Distance:** 135 miles | 2–2.5 hours
- **Route:** NV-169 west → I-15 north → UT-9
- **Camp:**
  - **Smithsonian Butte Road dispersed:** Off UT-59 south of Hurricane. 5-mile dirt road. Free BLM, expansive views of Zion and Canaan Mountain.
  - **Warner Valley Road dispersed:** BLM south of Hurricane off Warner Valley Rd. Free, easy 2WD access.
- **Gas:** Las Vegas (full services, fill here). Cedar City UT (if routing that way).

**Day 6 (Apr 9) — Valley of Fire Rest Day / Hike**
- *(If staying extra night at Valley of Fire before moving to Hurricane on Day 5 or staying near Zion)*
- **Activities:** Fire Wave trail, Mouse's Tank Canyon, White Domes loop

**Day 7 (Apr 10) — Gooseberry Mesa Riding Day** 🚵
- **Camp:** Hurricane/Gooseberry area

  - Access: Gooseberry Mesa Road off UT-59, 10 miles west of Hurricane. F150 is fine.

- **Camp:** BLM dispersed along Hwy 313 (see Route C staging notes)

## ❌ Route D: Idaho Loop — NOT RECOMMENDED

**Don't do this route for this trip. Here's why:**

- **Distance:** 1,400+ miles to Moab — most driving, least riding
- **Weather:** Nights 25–35°F, snow possible at Craters of the Moon (6,000 ft). Cold camping with no payoff.
- **Biking:** 1–2 days max. Craters of the Moon has minimal MTB. City of Rocks is a climbing destination, not biking.
- **Net result:** You arrive in Moab exhausted after 7 days of driving in the cold with 1 riding day under your belt.

**Idaho is a legitimate destination for a different trip** — ideally late June/July when you can hit Boise foothills, Wood River trails near Sun Valley, and Schweitzer area. Not early April on a biking-focused trip.

*If you're still curious: Craters of the Moon Loop Road (7-mile scenic drive), Shoshone Falls near Twin Falls, City of Rocks rock formations. But save it.*

### TL;DR
Exit White Rim, drive 30 minutes to Moab. Get a hotel room. Sleep. Next day: spa, food, optional light ride. Then split the drive home over two manageable days via US-50. **This is the right call after 3 days on White Rim.**

### Itinerary

**Day 1 (Apr 15) — Exit White Rim (Shafer Trail) → Moab**
- **Distance:** ~30 miles | 1 hour (Shafer Trail descent to Moab)
- **Lodging:** 🏨 **GET A HOTEL.** After 3 days on White Rim, you are filthy, sore, and running low on everything. A hotel is non-negotiable for morale and recovery. Budget $120–180/night.
  - **Options:** Moab Valley Inn, Gravity Haus Moab, La Quinta, Moab Springs Ranch
  - **Book in advance** — April is busy season in Moab.
- **Activities:** Shower (long one), laundry, real food at a restaurant, sleep
- **Note:** Don't camp on Day 1 post-White Rim. Just don't.

**Day 2 (Apr 16) — Moab Recovery**
- **Lodging:** Same hotel OR move to BLM dispersed if budget-conscious
- **Morning:** Arches Spa & Fitness, or just sleep in
- **Afternoon:** Moab Brewery lunch, City Market resupply, gear cleanup
- **Optional evening:** **Bar M Loop** (🟢 Easy, 7.8 mi) if legs feel good — mellow spin to remember why you love riding. Or: Arches NP scenic drive (easy, no hiking required).
- **Key rule:** Don't push. You'll be surprised how tired you actually are.

**Day 3 (Apr 17) — Moab → Ely, NV** ⚠️ LONG DAY
- **Distance:** 350 miles | 5.5–6 hours
- **Route:** US-191 north → I-70 west → US-50 west ("Loneliest Road in America")
- **Camp/Stay:** Motel in Ely ($65–85/night, Hotel Nevada is local/fun) OR BLM dispersed south of Ely off US-93 (Cave Lake area, ~5 miles from town)
- **Gas:** Fill in Moab. **CRITICAL:** Fill again in Green River UT (65 miles from Moab on I-70). Next reliable gas: Ely NV (280 miles from Moab). Don't gamble with fuel on US-50.
- **💧 WATER WARNING:** Carry 3 gallons. US-50 between Delta UT and Ely NV = 145 miles with minimal services.
- **Activities:** Drive day. Podcasts. You're going home.

**Day 4 (Apr 18) — Ely → Santa Cruz** ⚠️ LONG DAY
- **Distance:** 400 miles | 6–6.5 hours
- **Route:** US-50 west → US-395 south OR US-50 → I-80 west → Santa Cruz
- **Gas:** Fill in Ely. Next: Fallon NV (140 miles), then Reno/Sacramento area (full services).
- **Activities:** Final push home. Arrive evening.

> ⚠️ **DAYS 3–4 REALITY CHECK:** 750 miles when you're already tired from White Rim. This is unavoidable geography. US-50 through Nevada is remote and beautiful in a desolate way — audiobooks, podcasts, and a good playlist make it bearable. Stop every 2 hours.
