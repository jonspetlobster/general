# Camp Menu Plan — April 2026 Roadtrip

**Party:** 4 (2 adults, 2 kids)  
**Focus:** High protein, lower carb  
**Equipment:** Propane griddle, Ninja air fryer, stovetop, microwave, dutch oven, fridge, freezer  
**Oil:** Extra virgin olive oil throughout — on the griddle, in soups, on veg

---

## 🛒 Pre-Trip Todos
- [ ] Get frying pan or griddle (confirm what's in the trailer)
- [ ] Replace batteries (check/replace all: remotes, lanterns, etc.)
- [ ] Test trailer lock (before trip)
- [ ] Fill propane tanks before leaving
- [ ] Pre-boil 2 dozen eggs at home before leaving
- [ ] Prep and freeze: meatballs + sauce (2 batches), sausage, burger patties, chicken wings
- [ ] Stop at a Latin/Mexican place en route for pulled pork + arepas (plan into Day 10 stop)

---

## Protein Snack Stash
Keep these accessible the entire trip:
- Hard boiled eggs (pre-boil 2 dozen at home)
- Cottage cheese cups (high protein, great on their own or as a topping)
- String cheese / Babybel
- Beef jerky
- Mixed nuts
- Greek yogurt cups
- Fresh berries (blueberries, strawberries — restock at grocery stops)
- Rx Bars or similar (real ingredients)

---

## Freezer Prep (Do at Home)
Freeze these before leaving — thaw in order:
- Meatballs + sauce — 2 batches (Night 2 and Night 9)
- Sausage links/patties — freeze flat, thaw as needed
- Ground beef burger patties — flatten into pairs, freeze stacked
- Chicken wings — freeze raw, thaw 1-2 days before wing night

---

## Breakfast Setup
Breakfast isn't planned day-by-day — just rotate through what sounds good and what's quick.

**Options to have on hand:**
- Yogurt + berries + granola *(no-cook, fastest option)*
- Breakfast burritos — eggs, sausage, potato, cheese in a tortilla on the griddle
- Eggs + sausage on the griddle
- Pancakes (with sausage on the side)
- Cereal (backup / kid-friendly easy morning)
- PBJ or granola bars if breaking camp and need to move fast

**Prep notes:**
- Keep a bag of diced frozen potatoes for burritos (thaw overnight in fridge)
- Eggs always available — griddle is the workhorse here
- Granola + yogurt = zero cleanup

---

## Lunch Setup
Lunches are flexible. Keep these stocked:
- **PBJ** — bread, peanut butter, jelly. Always works, kids love it.
- **Greek yogurt cups** — protein punch, no prep
- Berries on the side
- Quesadillas on the griddle if there's time and a fire going
- Leftovers from the night before
- Road snacks on drive days: jerky, nuts, hard boiled eggs, string cheese, berries

## Phase 1: Outbound (Apr 4–11)

### Day 1 (Apr 4, Sat) — Big drive day
- **Breakfast:** Home, eat before leaving
- **Lunch:** Packed cooler — quesadilla wraps (chicken, cheese, cold), string cheese, jerky, berries
- **Dinner:** Chipotle bowls (high protein, skip the chips) or fast casual on the road

### Day 2 (Apr 5, Sun) — First real camp
- **Breakfast:** Eggs + sausage on griddle (EVOO)
- **Lunch:** PBJ + yogurt + berries
- **Dinner:** Meatballs + sauce in dutch oven *(make big batch, save leftovers)*

### Day 3 (Apr 6, Mon) — Riding day (Alabama Hills or Death Valley)
- **Breakfast:** Yogurt + berries + granola *(quick, no-cook)*
- **Lunch:** Meatball leftovers + salad + string cheese
- **Dinner:** Air fryer chicken thighs + roasted vegetables (EVOO, salt, garlic)

### Day 4 (Apr 7, Tue) — Drive to next camp
- **Breakfast:** Eggs + sausage on griddle
- **Lunch:** PBJ + yogurt
- **Dinner:** Sausage + spinach soup in dutch oven *(add white beans for extra protein)*

### Day 5 (Apr 8, Wed) — Riding day (Mammoth or Valley of Fire)
- **Breakfast:** Breakfast burritos — eggs, sausage, potato, cheese in a tortilla
- **Lunch:** Soup leftovers + berries
- **Dinner:** 🍗 **Wing night** — air fryer chicken wings (22 min, incredibly crispy, zero effort). Buffalo or garlic parmesan. Kids go crazy for these.

### Day 6 (Apr 9, Thu) — Long drive or rest day
- **Breakfast:** Pancakes + sausage on griddle
- **Lunch:** Quesadillas on griddle + cottage cheese on the side
- **Dinner:** 🍕 **Calzone night** in dutch oven *(homemade dough — mix before leaving or early AM to rise. Kids build their own: sausage, ricotta, cottage cheese, mozzarella)*

### Day 7 (Apr 10, Fri) — Gooseberry Mesa riding
- **Breakfast:** Yogurt + granola + berries
- **Lunch:** PBJ + yogurt + string cheese
- **Dinner:** 🌯 **Burrito bowls** — seasoned ground beef or chicken, rice (instant or microwave), beans, cheese, salsa, sour cream. Everyone builds their own.

### Day 8 (Apr 11, Sat) — Drive to Grand Staircase-Escalante
- **Breakfast:** Eggs + sausage on griddle — fuel up
- **Lunch:** Road snacks — nuts, jerky, hard boiled eggs, berries, string cheese
- **Dinner:** Smash burgers on griddle + coleslaw *(adults bunless, kids get buns)* — first night at GSENM, celebrate the new camp

## Phase 2: Grand Staircase-Escalante (Apr 11–14)

Base camp cooking — you're set up and staying put. Lean into the dutch oven and air fryer.

### Day 9 (Apr 12, Sun) — Devil's Garden + Calf Creek Falls
- **Breakfast:** Breakfast burritos — eggs, sausage, potato, cheese *(cook while everyone wakes up)*
- **Lunch:** PBJ + yogurt + berries, pack extra for trail
- **Dinner:** Meatballs + sauce in dutch oven (thawed batch #2) — big, hearty, easy after a long hike

### Day 10 (Apr 13, Mon) — Slot canyon day
- **Breakfast:** Eggs + sausage on griddle *(big breakfast before a physical day)*
- **Lunch:** Packed wraps — turkey/salami, cheese, mustard. Berries on the side. Or PBJ if keeping it simple.
- **Dinner:** 🫓 **Pulled pork + arepas** *(picked up from a Latin/Mexican stop on the way)*. Serve with whatever fixings you grabbed — salsa, slaw, cheese.

### Day 11 (Apr 14, Tue) — Kodachrome Basin + drive toward Capitol Reef
- **Breakfast:** Yogurt + berries + granola *(no-cook, break camp efficiently)*
- **Lunch:** On the road — Escalante town stop or packed snacks
- **Dinner:** Chicken soup in dutch oven at Capitol Reef *(rotisserie chicken from Escalante market, shred into broth with veg)*

## Phase 3: Return (Apr 15–18)

Energy is lower. Keep it simple and satisfying.

### Day 12 (Apr 15, Wed) — Capitol Reef / first return leg
- **Breakfast:** Cereal or yogurt (low-effort morning)
- **Lunch:** Leftovers or cottage cheese + berries + jerky
- **Dinner:** Burrito bowls round 2 or sausage + spinach soup — warming, easy, zero effort

### Day 13 (Apr 16, Thu) — Drive day
- **Breakfast:** Grab-and-go — granola bars, yogurt, cereal
- **Lunch:** Road snacks
- **Dinner:** Air fryer chicken thighs + roasted veg (EVOO) + coleslaw if any left

### Day 14 (Apr 17, Fri) — Last camp or drive home
- **Breakfast:** Fridge clear-out scramble — all remaining eggs, meat, veg on the griddle in EVOO
- **Lunch:** Road snacks
- **Dinner:** Home or motel

## 🔥 Desserts

### Dutch Oven Desserts
| Dessert | Effort | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Peach or berry cobbler** | Low | Canned peaches + boxed cake mix + butter on top. Dump and bake. Classic. |
| **Cinnamon rolls** | Medium | Pillsbury tube — let them rise in the DO, 25 min with coals on top and bottom. Kids go wild. |
| **Brownies** | Low | Boxed mix + water + oil + eggs. 30 min. Serve warm from the pot. |
| **Bread pudding** | Medium | Day-old bread + eggs + milk + cinnamon + raisins. Comfort food. |

### Campfire Desserts
| Dessert | Effort | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **S'mores** | None | Classic. Always a hit. Bring extra marshmallows. |
| **Banana boats** | None | Split banana, stuff with chocolate chips + mini marshmallows, wrap in foil, 5 min in coals. |
| **Campfire cones** | None | Waffle cones packed with chocolate chips, mini marshmallows, crushed graham. Wrap in foil, heat on grate 5 min. |
| **Roasted apples** | Low | Core an apple, fill with brown sugar + butter + cinnamon, wrap in foil, 15-20 min in coals. |

**Recommendation:** Peach cobbler on Night 2 (settling in, first big camp). Cinnamon rolls as a special morning treat. Banana boats or campfire cones for quick nightly hits — kids can do them themselves.

### Air Fryer Tips
- **Wings:** 380°F, 22 min, flip halfway. No oil needed.
- **Chicken thighs:** 400°F, 22-25 min. Bone-in = juicier.
- **Reheating:** Air fryer beats microwave every time — meatballs, quesadillas, calzone pieces all come back crispy.

### Cottage Cheese Uses
- Snack: With berries + nuts — high protein, no cooking
- Calzone filling: Mix with mozzarella, higher protein than ricotta alone
- Twice baked potato filling: Swaps in for sour cream seamlessly
- Scrambled eggs: Stir in a scoop — creamier, kids won't notice
- Side: Single-serve cups travel well in cooler
