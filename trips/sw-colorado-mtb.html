SW Colorado Mountain Bike Trip

Spring/Summer 2026 • 2-5 Day Options • Durango-to-Moab Style Terrain

Option 1: Colorado Trail - Durango Section (3-4 days)

Trail Info →
Distance: 75 miles
Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced
Season: June-September
Highlights: High alpine meadows, technical descents, stunning San Juan views
Routing: Durango → Molas Pass (camp), Molas → Silverton (hotel), Silverton → Durango via shuttle or loop back
Why: Classic Colorado singletrack, mix of challenging climbs and flowy descents, good resupply options in Silverton

Option 2: Kokopelli Trail (4-5 days)

Trail Info (BLM) → MTB Project →
Distance: 142 miles (Loma, CO → Moab, UT)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Season: March-May, September-October
Highlights: Red rock desert, Slickrock sections, camping under stars
Routing: Loma → Mary's Loop → Kokopelli proper → Sand Flats → Moab
Why: Epic desert riding, point-to-point adventure, ends in Moab for brewery celebration

Option 3: San Juan Hut System (3-5 days)

Official Site & Reservations →
Distance: 215 miles total (can do sections)
Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced
Season: June-September
Highlights: Hut-to-hut riding (no tent needed!), high alpine passes, wildflowers
Routing: Telluride → Ridgway → Ouray (or reverse). Can cherry-pick 3-day section: Last Dollar → Deep Creek → Burn
Why: Comfortable huts with bunks/stoves, supported riding (gear stays at huts), less gear to carry

Option 4: Hermosa Creek Trail (2-3 days)

Trail Info (MTB Project) → Shuttle Service →
Distance: 21 miles one-way (can shuttle or loop)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Season: June-September
Highlights: Creek-side singletrack, wildflowers, forest riding, very flowy
Routing: Durango base camp → shuttle to Purgatory → ride down Hermosa Creek → camp at creek → ride out or loop back via roads
Why: Shorter commitment, classic Durango trail, great for mixed skill levels, easy logistics

Option 5: Monarch Crest Trail (2-3 days)

Shuttle Service → Guided Tours →
Distance: 35-40 miles (loop or shuttle)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Season: July-September
Highlights: Above-treeline ridge riding, 360° views, technical descents
Routing: Salida base camp → shuttle to Monarch Pass → ride Crest Trail → descend Rainbow Trail → Salida
Why: One of Colorado's best high-alpine rides, manageable distance, Salida has great food/breweries

Option 6: Phil's World + Whole Enchilada Combo (3 days)

Phil's World (BLM) → Whole Enchilada Shuttle →
Distance: Varies (20-30 mi/day)
Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced
Season: April-May, September-October
Highlights: Desert classics, shuttle-able epics, Moab culture
Itinerary: Day 1: Cortez → Phil's World (local favorite, flowy)
Day 2: Drive to Moab, ride Mag 7 or Captain Ahab
Day 3: Whole Enchilada (shuttle-assisted 13k' descent)
Why: Hit multiple regions, mix of terrain, can add/drop days easily

Logistics Notes

  • Shuttle services: Durango, Moab, Crested Butte all have bike shuttle companies
  • Camping: Most trails have established sites or BLM dispersed camping
  • Resupply: Silverton, Ridgway, Moab have bike shops + food
  • Vehicle logistics: Some routes need 2-car shuttle or paid shuttle service
